## Overview

The AquaPure electronic salt chlorine generator produces chlorine from a low
concentration of salt dissolved in the pool water. The system consists of the
Power Center (PLC), the electrolytic cell, and a flow switch. The recommended
salt level is 3,000–3,500 ppm, with 3,200 ppm ideal.

## Operation

The Power Center display shows the current salt reading, water temperature,
cell voltage, and any active service code. Use the Up/Down buttons to set the
chlorine production percentage. The system only produces chlorine when adequate
water flow is detected by the flow switch.

## Service Codes

Service codes appear on the Power Center display to indicate operating
conditions that need attention. Resolve the underlying condition; the code
clears automatically once normal operation resumes.

### Service Code 120 — Low Salt

Salt level is below the operating range. Add pool-grade salt per the dosing
chart and allow it to dissolve fully with the pump running before re-testing.

### Service Code 121 — Very Low Salt

Salt level is critically low and chlorine production has stopped. Add salt and
re-test. Do not run the cell until the salt level returns to range.

### Service Code 122 — Low Water Temperature

Water temperature is below the minimum for chlorine production. Production
resumes automatically when the water warms. No action is required.

### Service Code 124 — High Salt

Salt level is above the operating range. Partially drain and refill with fresh
water to dilute, then re-test.

### Service Code 125 — Check Flow / No Flow Detected

The flow switch is not detecting adequate water flow through the cell. Confirm
the pump is running, clean the pump and skimmer baskets, check for a clogged
filter, and ensure all valves are open. If the code persists with confirmed
flow, the flow switch may need inspection by a service professional.

### Service Code 170 — Inspect Cell

The cell voltage is outside the expected range, often due to scale buildup or a
cell approaching end of life. Inspect and clean the cell per the cleaning
instructions; replace the cell if readings do not return to normal.

## Cell Cleaning

Power off the system before removing the cell. Inspect for white scale deposits.
If present, use a manufacturer-approved cell cleaning solution and rinse with a
hose. Never use a metal object to scrape the plates.

## Chemical Safety

Add chemicals to the pool water separately and never mix pool chemicals
together. Combining products such as acid with chlorine can release toxic gas.
Always follow the product label and store chemicals in their original
containers, away from each other.

## Specifications

Recommended salt: 3,000–3,500 ppm (3,200 ppm ideal). Operating water
temperature: above 50°F (10°C). Power: 120/240 VAC. Cell life: typically
3–5 seasons depending on use and water balance.
