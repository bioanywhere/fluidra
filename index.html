02 · Executive summary

One bet: ship a narrow assistant pool owners can trust, then earn the right to widen it.

In 90 days we launch a Gemini-powered assistant inside the Fluidra Pool app that resolves the moments owners actually struggle with, equipment fault codes, water care, routines, product discovery, grounded in Fluidra's own documentation, with hard rails around chemistry dosing and repair. Trust is the launch metric; everything else compounds from it. And one proof of method up front: ecosystem research re-sequenced this roadmap, the wedge is troubleshooting, not chemistry. Evidence over opinion.

Strategic thinking

Narrow MVP, deep quality

5 use cases in across 90 days, 4 deliberately out. The wedge: equipment troubleshooting, cleanest knowledge, lowest risk, highest support-call deflection, earning trust before chemistry ships.

Technical credibility

RAG over real manuals

Gemini on Vertex AI + retrieval over Fluidra's official documentation, named manuals, enumerable fault codes (JXi, AquaPure, TruClear, Polaris iQ). Grounded, citable, auditable, updatable in hours.

Risk awareness

Guardrails are the product

Chlorine + acid makes chlorine gas; trichlor + cal-hypo can ignite. These are documented incidents, not hypotheticals. Risk is classified before generation; the guardrail trigger rate is a launch gate.

Fluidra is actively buying its way into the "Internet of Pools", and the competitive scan shows the white space: Pentair and Hayward pair hardware with rule-based dosing; pHin and Sutro monitor chemistry. Nobody offers a natural-language assistant grounded in official documentation that troubleshoots fault codes, advises water care, and drives parts discovery. The assistant's job is to put Fluidra's documentation moat where the owner already is, at the moment of doubt:

Stated assumptions

① Pilot on the Fluidra Pool app in one EMEA market, it's newer, already GDPR-compliant, and already surfaces rule-based recommendations and Blue Connect / AquaConnect sensor data we can read (not control). ② English first, architected for multilingual. ③ Legal & brand reviewers part-time. ④ No device control in MVP; sensor context is read-only. ⑤ Support team can receive warm handoffs.

What the field data says

Owner demand clusters tightly: green water = chlorine collapse; cloudy = filtration/pH; "chlorine that won't hold" = the FC:CYA ratio almost nobody understands (trichlor tabs silently raise CYA all season); pump won't prime ≈ water level + suction air leak; salt cells throw enumerable codes. High-volume, answerable from official docs, ideal first territory.

Internal alpha. Product team + 5 Fluidra support agents red-team daily, they know which codes generate calls. Triage failures: KB gap vs. prompt gap vs. guardrail gap.

06 · Phase two, Hardening
DAY 31 → 60

Chemistry earns its way in, behind a proven guardrail stack.

Goal: water-chemistry guidance ships only once the safety stack is battle-tested on the wedge, then 200–500 real owners in a closed beta with a live escalation path.
Wk 5–6
Safety depth, then chemistry. Risk-tier classifier in front of generation. Structured dosing flow: pool volume + test reading mandatory, validated tables (FC 2–4 ppm · pH 7.4–7.6 · TA 80–120 · CYA 30–50, salt pools 60–80), FC:CYA logic built in, fixed handling warnings. Hard block on mixing queries. Gate: zero guardrail false-negatives in red-team, or chemistry holds.
Wk 7
Quality loop + sensor context. Nightly evals (groundedness, refusal accuracy both directions, tone). Read-only Blue Connect / AquaConnect readings personalize answers ("your pH has been 7.9 for three days, that's why the chlorine isn't holding"). Maintenance routines ship. Designer rewrites the worst flows weekly from transcripts.
Wk 8
Closed beta. Pilot-market cohort live on the Fluidra Pool app. Instrument everything: deflection, troubleshooting success rate, CSAT, retrieval hit-rate, escalation rate, guardrail trigger rate, latency, cost per conversation.

≥92% golden-set pass · 100% of dosing via structured flow · 100% of mixing probes blocked · troubleshooting success ≥70% · beta CSAT ≥4.0 · zero severity-1 incidents.

Why RAG, not fine-tuning

Grounded, citable to the exact manual section, auditable, updatable in hours. Source hierarchy enforced: official manuals and the Helpshift KB are authoritative; forums and third-party mirrors inform intent analysis only, never answers.

Why safety pre-model

Classifying risk before generation routes dangerous intents to deterministic flows or humans. We never rely on the LLM to police itself, the red path above bypasses generation entirely when needed.

Why evals from week 3

With three people, the eval harness is the scaling mechanism: every prompt, KB or model change ships only if the golden set stays green. Tier 2/3 regressions block deploys automatically.

09 · Safety & compliance

Three tiers. One rule: when risk is physical, the assistant gets less creative.

Tier 1 · Answer freely

Informational

Routines, how products work, water-care concepts, product discovery. Grounded generation with citations to Fluidra documentation.

Tier 2 · Structured only

Chemistry & dosing

No free-form dosing, ever. Volume + test reading required; computed from validated tables with FC:CYA logic; fixed warnings on every answer. The hazards are documented, not hypothetical: chlorine + acid releases chlorine gas; trichlor + cal-hypo can ignite. Mixing queries are hard-blocked pre-generation.

Tier 3 · Diagnose & escalate

Equipment · gas · electrical · health

Fault-code lookup and isolate-the-cause only. Gas heaters carry CO/flue risk; salt cells without flow can build hydrogen; wiring is NEC/CEC territory. Beyond diagnosis → immediate routing to Fluidra Pro / dealer network or support, with warm context transfer.

User question → Risk classifierIntent + entities + tier, decided before any generation
↓ routes to one of three paths
Tier 1Grounded answer + citation to the manual
Tier 2Structured flow, deterministic calculator
Tier 3Human / professional, warm handoff
Every path loggedAudit trail + nightly red-team evals

Compliance controls

GDPR by design, the Fluidra Pool app is already GDPR-compliant, the assistant inherits that bar; CCPA for US expansion. PII redaction before logging · regional disclaimer patterns (chemistry codes vary, e.g. CDC's Model Aquatic Health Code) · full audit trail · legal sign-off on every Tier 2/3 template · incident runbook · global kill-switch.

Failure-mode management

Refusal accuracy is tracked in both directions, over-refusal erodes trust as surely as under-refusal creates risk. An adversarial red-team suite runs nightly; any Tier 2/3 regression blocks the deploy automatically.
